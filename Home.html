Currently, I am eager to expand my knowledge and experience in astrophysics. I am open to opportunities that will allow me to learn new skills and explore things I am passionate about. I plan to focus on higher education after graduation, and I hope to pursue a career in academia or research in the future.

Projects

Sophomore Research Seminar: Measuring the Beam Asymmetry for π+π− → Ks and pπ0 → Σ+

  • Learned about the fundamentals of particle physics and relativistic kinematics, including identifying and classifying different types of particles.
  • Gained knowledge of basic data analysis techniques using C++ to subtract background, identify particles, and fit mass distributions.
  • Studied the decay of neutral kaons using data collected from the GlueX experiment at Jefferson Lab in Virginia.

Final Project for PHY-310 (Computational Physics): Modeling Fit Parameters of Cosmological Models using Type Ia Supernova Data

  • Made use of Type Ia supernova data from the Supernova Cosmology Project to fit cosmological parameters
  • Applied different numerical methods including Simpson’s method and multivariable adaptable stepping minimization function on the reduced chi-squared fitting between the observation and the theoretical model for data fitting

Final Project for AST-230 (Observational Astronomy): Spin Period of AO Psc

  • Observed the target, an intermediate polar type cataclysmic variable star AO Psc, for two nights, one with UCO and the other through a remote telescope.
  • Analyzed CCD data from AO Psc and constructed a light curve displaying the variability of the binary star system after plate-solving and finding comparison stars using an RA-Dec overlay made from stars with known brightness
